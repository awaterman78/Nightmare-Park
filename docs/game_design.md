# Game Design, Nightmare Park, Monster Royale

## One line pitch

A haunted real time monster battler where players deploy creepy creatures anywhere in their half of the arena and try to destroy the enemy core.

## Design pillars

### 1. Monsters must feel alive

Every monster needs personality, movement, weight, anticipation, attack rhythm, hit reaction and death.

The player should enjoy deploying a monster even before the wider game is complete.

### 2. Placement matters

Unlike rigid lane-only play, the player can place monsters anywhere in their half.

Placement should influence:

- Which bridge they take.
- Which tower they approach.
- Whether they intercept enemies.
- Whether they reach the core safely.

### 3. Clear, readable combat

Even with premium art, the player must instantly understand:

- What they selected.
- Where they can place it.
- What it is attacking.
- Whether it is winning or dying.
- What the next decision should be.

### 4. Haunted but not muddy

The tone is spooky, gothic, funny and stylish. It should not become visually unreadable.

Use strong silhouettes, bright ability effects and clear health bars.

## Core gameplay loop

1. Energy refills over time.
2. Player selects a monster card.
3. Player places the monster anywhere in their half.
4. Monster automatically moves, targets and attacks.
5. Enemy deploys counters.
6. Player reacts with placement and card timing.
7. Towers and cores take damage.
8. Destroy enemy core to win.

## First vertical slice

Only build:

- Arena One.
- Grave Goblin.
- One enemy dummy deployment system.
- Player deployment anywhere in own half.
- Basic tower/core damage.
- Basic card and energy UI.

The aim is to prove the feel before adding the full roster.
